1. Long-term / Strategic Jobs
A. Define and govern cyber / InfoSec strategy

Set the overarching security strategy and program based on risk assessments and best practices, and communicate it to executives, board and other stakeholders. 
Translate strategy into a cyber governance model (policies, standards, control frameworks like NIST/ISO, ownership) within IT Risk Management and IT Compliance tooling. 
B. Build an integrated cyber GRC architecture

Decide how to unify IT risk, IT compliance, vendor risk and ERM into a single GRC view (Diligent One + ITRM + IT Compliance + IT Vendor Risk + ERM). 
Define data model, ownership and workflows so that risk, control and incident data roll up into board-ready dashboards and reports (including Diligent Board Reporting for IT Risk). 
C. Design third-party / ecosystem risk program

Establish the third-party and supply-chain risk management program: segmentation, due diligence, continuous monitoring, remediation rules, and escalation thresholds using IT Vendor Risk Management and external ratings (Bitsight, SecurityScorecard). 
D. Embed cyber into enterprise risk and board governance

Integrate cyber and IT risk into the enterprise risk framework and appetite, including how it’s presented in ERM and at the board. 
Define the board engagement model: cadence of cyber briefings, standard KPIs, scenario-planning, and how cyber maps to business value and resilience, supported by Cyber Leadership Playbook guidance. 
E. Program-level resilience & incident capability

Architect incident response, crisis management and resilience programs (including third-party and AI-driven threats), with clear RACI across CISO, GC, operations and business owners. 
2. Monthly / Quarterly Jobs
A. Cyber / IT risk management cycle

Update IT risk registers and assessments, including likelihood/impact scoring, trends and scenario analysis in IT Risk Management and ERM. 
Review risk metrics: control effectiveness, residual risk, exceptions, open issues, and map them to business processes and assets. 
B. Compliance and control assurance

Run and review IT compliance status against key regimes (ISO, SOC, NIST, DORA, etc.), using IT Compliance to map controls, track attestations, evidence and deadlines. 
Coordinate audits and control testing (internal audit, external assessments) and ensure findings are logged, owned and tracked to closure across the GRC platform. 
C. Vendor and third-party reviews

Review vendor risk posture changes from continuous monitoring (score movements, new findings), decide on remediation, additional controls or termination, and document decisions. 
Re-assess critical suppliers on a periodic cadence (questionnaires, evidence reviews, contract obligations). 
D. Executive and board-level reporting

Prepare quarterly (or more frequent) cyber and IT risk reports: standardized dashboards, trend lines, top risks, incidents, third-party exposure and planned mitigations, leveraging Diligent Board Reporting for IT Risk. 
Align with GC and other GRC leaders to present a unified risk and compliance narrative to the board and regulators. 
3. Weekly / Day-to-Day Jobs
A. Continuous risk and control monitoring

Monitor alerts and changes from continuous cyber, IT control and third-party monitoring (vulnerabilities, failed controls, degrading vendor scores) and turn them into tickets, risks or incidents in the GRC platform. 
Keep risk and compliance records current: status updates on mitigations, exceptions, waivers, compensating controls and due dates. 
B. Compliance operations

Manage task queues for control owners: upcoming attestations, evidence requests, policy acknowledgements and remediation steps across IT Compliance and ITRM. 
Support security audits and customer DDQs by aggregating evidence once and re-using it for multiple stakeholders (internal audit, customers, regulators). 
C. Stakeholder communication and enablement

Brief executives and business owners on current cyber risk posture, key open issues, and trade-offs, in business language rather than technical jargon. 
Prepare or update board presentation materials for upcoming meetings: concise narratives, “so-what” impact, and clear mitigation plans. 
D. Product / project and architecture partnering

Work with product, engineering and IT teams to ensure new initiatives meet security requirements and risk is captured in the central risk/compliance tooling. 
4. Emergencies / Incidents & CISO Role
These are the high-urgency situations your dashboard should surface aggressively.

A. Major cyber incident (e.g., ransomware, breach, disruptive attack)

Typical jobs:

Rapidly detect and triage the event; confirm impacted systems, data, vendors and regions. 
Stand up incident command, coordinate with GC, operations, comms and relevant vendors; ensure actions follow predefined playbooks and regulatory requirements. 
Use IT Risk / IT Compliance / Vendor Risk and ERM data to quantify exposure and articulate business impact to executives and the board in near real time. 
CISO’s role: incident co-leader and chief risk translator — turning technical chaos into business and board-ready decisions.

B. Third-party / supply-chain incident

Typical jobs:

Identify affected vendors and dependencies, using vendor risk monitoring and mappings. 
Decide on containment actions (disable integrations, enhanced monitoring, contract remedies) and track them in the platform. 
Coordinate notifications and reporting to customers, regulators and the board about third-party exposure. 
C. Regulatory or compliance crisis

Typical jobs:

Respond rapidly to regulator inquiries or enforcement actions around cyber, privacy or operational resilience (e.g., missed obligations, inadequate controls). 
Use centralized IT risk / compliance evidence to prove due diligence, governance and remediation progress. 
D. Internal whistleblower / fraud-related security concerns

Typical jobs:

Investigate allegations that touch cyber controls or data security, coordinating with legal and ethics/compliance teams. 
Ensure independent, well-documented handling and translate findings into control improvements and board-level updates where material. 
In all these scenarios, the CISO and their team are responsible for fast situational awareness across assets, vendors, controls and obligations and for turning that into clear, defensible decisions and communications — exactly the workflow Diligent’s CISO / InfoSec solutions are designed to support.
