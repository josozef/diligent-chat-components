Here’s a frequency-based view of the core **jobs to be done** for a corporate secretary (CoSec) and governance team, grounded in how Diligent describes the role and workflows.

---

### 1. Long-term / Annual & Strategic Projects

**A. Design and maintain the governance framework**

- Define and periodically refresh the **corporate governance framework** (board and committee structure, charters, delegation of authority, policies).  
- Lead or co-lead **governance policy development** (e.g., board governance policy, code of conduct, conflict of interest, ESG oversight), ensure board approval, and oversee implementation and updates.  
- Map **legal entity governance**: articles, bylaws, shareholder agreements, committee charters, and ensure directors act within those instruments across parent and subsidiaries.  

**B. Entity management and corporate structure**

- Maintain the **legal entity register** and group structure (subsidiaries, JVs, holdings), including incorporations, dissolutions, mergers and structural simplifications.  
- Oversee entity-level compliance calendars (statutory filings, licenses, local director requirements, beneficial ownership registers, FAR/ECCTA-style regimes, etc.).  

**C. Board and director capability**

- Design and run **board and director evaluation** processes (self-assessments, peer reviews, committee reviews) and follow-up action plans.  
- Own **director onboarding and ongoing training** on governance, risk, regulatory changes, and strategic topics; track completion and readiness.  
- Support **succession planning** (board, committee chairs, often C-suite) and skills matrix tracking.  

**D. Major corporate events**

- Coordinate **M&A transactions, IPOs and capital markets events** from a governance/board perspective (approvals, documentation, due diligence data, disclosure, filings).  
- Support **significant policy overhauls** (e.g., corporate governance policy refresh, ESG framework, risk appetite statement) with board and stakeholder engagement.  

---

### 2. Recurring Monthly / Quarterly Jobs

**A. Board and committee meeting cycle**

For each board and committee (audit, risk, nominations/governance, compensation, etc.):

- Maintain the **annual board and committee calendar**, with all statutory and recurring agenda items pre-mapped.  
- Draft and iterate **agendas** with the chair and CEO/GC/CFO; ensure coverage of fiduciary duties and regulatory obligations.  
- Coordinate **board packs**: collect and QA management reports, committee reports, and external materials; publish via board portal.  
- Manage **attendance, quorum, and logistics** (rooms/VC, presentations, tech needs, secure access).  

Post-meeting:

- Draft, review and finalize **minutes** as the official record of deliberations and decisions; route for approval and archive to the corporate record.  
- Track **action items and resolutions**, ensuring management follow-through before the next cycle.  

**B. Statutory and regulatory filings**

- Prepare and file **periodic regulatory reports** and corporate filings (e.g., annual returns, director and officer updates, listing rules submissions), often on a monthly/quarterly cadence depending on jurisdiction and listing status.  
- Coordinate **disclosure and reporting** with the CFO, GC and investor relations (financial releases, annual/quarterly reports, governance statements) for board approval.  

**C. Governance program upkeep**

- Periodic **review of governance policies and board procedures** to keep in line with evolving laws, codes and best practices.  
- Engage and oversee **governance service providers** (board portal, entity management, registered agents, shareholder services) and validate service quality and security.  

---

### 3. Weekly / Meeting-Cycle & Operational Jobs

**A. Continuous board support and information flow**

- Act as **first point of contact** for directors and executives for governance questions, board processes, and access to records.  
- Curate and distribute **regulatory and market intelligence** to keep the board informed (e.g., key enforcement trends, code changes, activism signals).  

**B. Record-keeping and entity data hygiene**

- Maintain **up-to-date corporate records**: minutes, resolutions, charters, policy versions, delegations of authority, shareholder registers, powers of attorney, etc.  
- Keep **entity data synchronized** across systems (board portal, entity management system, company registry filings, internal HR/finance systems).  

**C. Board and director lifecycle tasks**

- Handle **director logistics**: availability, travel, technology support, access management, and secure communications channels.  
- Manage **director education logistics** (scheduling sessions, tracking attendance, distributing materials, capturing feedback).  

**D. Internal collaboration**

- Coordinate regularly with **GC, CFO, CRO, CHRO and other executives** to ensure board materials are complete, accurate and aligned with strategy and risk profile.  
- Support **cross-functional initiatives** that have governance implications (e.g., risk assessments, ESG reporting, major policy changes).  

---

### 4. Ad-hoc Emergencies / Incidents & CoSec’s Role

These are less predictable but critical “jobs to be done” where the CoSec becomes a **governance incident commander**.

**A. Regulatory or legal incidents**

Typical triggers:

- Regulatory investigation, dawn raid, or urgent information request.  
- Discovery of material non-compliance (e.g., missed filings, sanctions breach, economic crime / corporate transparency issues).  
- Significant litigation or whistleblower allegations affecting the board or C-suite.

CoSec roles:

- Rapidly **convene the board or relevant committee**, ensuring proper notice and documentation.  
- Provide and control access to **complete and accurate records** for investigators, auditors, regulators and external counsel.  
- Ensure **proper process and minutes** for all emergency meetings to evidence that fiduciary duties and governance standards were followed.  

**B. Risk, cyber and operational crises**

Typical triggers:

- Major **cybersecurity breach or IT outage** impacting operations or sensitive data.  
- Significant **operational incident** (safety, environmental, product failure) with board-level implications.  

CoSec roles:

- Coordinate the **governance response**: which committees meet, in what sequence, with what information, and what approvals are needed for public statements and remediation plans.  
- Ensure **alignment between management crisis teams and the board**, including secure communication channels and clear documentation of decisions.  

**C. Board and leadership disruptions**

Typical triggers:

- Sudden **resignation, death or incapacity** of a director, chair, or key executive.  
- Conflicts of interest, conduct issues, or governance breakdowns at board level.

CoSec roles:

- Guide the board through **succession and appointment procedures** defined in bylaws and charters; coordinate nominations/governance committee as needed.  
- Ensure **conflict-of-interest policies** and recusal processes are followed and documented.  

**D. Shareholder and stakeholder pressure**

Typical triggers:

- **Shareholder activism**, hostile approaches, or contentious AGM items.  
- Reputational crises tied to ESG, ethics, or strategy.

CoSec roles:

- Orchestrate the **board’s governance response**: extra meetings, information packs, briefings, and documentation of deliberations and decisions.  
- Coordinate with IR, legal and communications on **disclosures and stakeholder messaging**, ensuring proper approvals and records.

### How this maps to your dashboard

Without going into design details, these frequency buckets translate well into:

- **“Today / This week”**: meeting prep and follow-ups, actions, filings due, director asks.  
- **“This month / Quarter”**: board and committee cycle, key filings, evaluations/training, policy reviews.  
- **“This year / Strategic”**: governance framework changes, structure and major transactions, program reviews.  
- **“On fire / Incident”**: a compact view for crises showing which boards/committees must convene, what filings or records are needed, and status of actions and approvals.

That structure should give you a solid backbone of jobs-to-be-done to anchor navigation, alerts and summaries across Diligent’s board and entity governance tools.
