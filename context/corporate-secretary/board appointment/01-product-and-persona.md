# 01 — Product Overview & User Persona

## What This App Does

This application automates the **director appointment process** for corporate governance teams. It guides a user through collecting the required data, routing a board resolution for approval, filing regulatory documents, and updating entity records — replacing what is normally a manual, multi-week, multi-system process.

The scope of this prototype is a **single workflow**: appointing a new director to one of several subsidiary companies. The workflow has four phases: **Identify Candidate** **Approval**, **Filing**, and **Entity Update**.

---

## Domain Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Entity** | A legal company registered in a specific jurisdiction (e.g. a Singapore Pte. Ltd., a Delaware LLC). The parent company manages a portfolio of these. |
| **Director** | A member of a company's board of directors. |
| **Appointment** | The legal act of adding a new individual to a company's board. |
| **Replacement** | A variant where a departing director is replaced by a new appointee. Adds a resignation record. |
| **Board Resolution** | A formal document recording the board's decision to appoint a director. Generated by the system, sent to board members for approval/signature. |
| **Consent to Act** | A legal form (e.g. Singapore's Form 45) where the appointee consents to serve as director. In some jurisdictions this is filed with the regulator. |
| **Filing** | Submitting required documents (signed resolution, consent form) to a regulatory body. In Singapore this is ACRA; elsewhere it varies. |
| **Approver** | A board member who must vote to approve the appointment. Organized by committee. |
| **Committee** | A standing board committee (e.g. Nomination, Audit). The user selects which committee's members will approve a given appointment. |

## User Persona

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Name** | Sarah |
| **Title** | Corporate Secretary / Governance Officer |
| **Employer** | Acme, Inc. (parent holding company) |
| **Responsibilities** | Manages board composition, governance compliance, and entity records across Acme's portfolio of 6 subsidiaries spanning 4 countries |
| **Pain Points** | Coordinating across jurisdictions, tracking approval status, ensuring regulatory deadlines, manual document preparation |
| **Tech Comfort** | Proficient with SaaS tools; expects modern, responsive UI; comfortable with AI assistance but wants control over final decisions |
| **Goal** | Reduce time-to-appointment, eliminate missed filings, maintain a clear audit trail |

Sarah manages entities in the US, Ireland, Netherlands, and Singapore. She needs to know what data is required, who needs to approve, and what documents to file — and she wants the system to handle as much of that as possible while keeping her in the loop.

## Core Design Principles (Interface-Agnostic)

1. **Guided process** — The user should always know what step they're on and what comes next. The system leads; the user confirms.
2. **Progressive data collection** — Collect only what's needed at each step. Don't ask for everything upfront.
3. **Re-present accumulated state** — After any sub-flow (e.g. adding a new person), return the user to the main flow with their previous selections visible and editable.
4. **Background orchestration** — Once the approval workflow starts, it runs autonomously. The user can monitor progress and intervene only when needed (e.g. confirming filing).
5. **Disable-after-commit** — Once a step is submitted, its controls become read-only. The flow is linear; users don't go back and silently change data.
6. **Jurisdiction-aware** — Document names, regulatory bodies, and filing instructions adapt to the selected company's jurisdiction.
